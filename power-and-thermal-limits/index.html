Reference layer · 06 Power and Thermal

Power and Thermal Limits

Compute capacity is bounded by the ability to deliver power and remove heat.

A chip or system that cannot be reliably energized and cooled is not deployable capacity. Power and thermal conditions therefore belong inside the sovereignty stack.

Reference framingThis page defines an analytical layer. It does not make a current operational claim about any entity.
01

Power as an operating condition

Assessment may include available capacity, density, connection timelines, redundancy, power quality, and the operating constraints of the intended deployment.

02

Thermal as an operating condition

Cooling method, heat rejection, facility design, environmental constraints, maintenance, and water requirements may affect practical deployment.

03

Scope before conclusion

Power and cooling conditions are location-, design-, and time-specific. Operational claims require current evidence and must distinguish planned capacity from available capacity.
