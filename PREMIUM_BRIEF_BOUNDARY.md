# Premium Brief Boundary

## Purpose

This document defines what governed premium outputs may include, what they must
exclude, and how evidence, confidence, scope, and as-of requirements apply.

It applies to Compute Sovereignty Briefs, Chip Dependency Audits, Premium
Reference Dossiers, and licensed uses of the ontology and methodology.

## Compute Sovereignty Brief

### May include

- scoped synthesis across declared sovereignty-stack layers;
- exposure-type framing where evidence supports it;
- separation of verified fact, attributed statement, estimate, analysis, and
  unknown;
- confidence labels, limitations, conflicts, and as-of dating;
- registry-linked source references within supported claim scope.

### Must not include

- sovereignty or non-sovereignty assignment for any entity;
- rankings, benchmarks, league tables, or comparative scores;
- investment, legal, procurement, or national-security advice;
- outcome guarantees or implied certification;
- vendor comparison presented as a purchase recommendation.

## Chip Dependency Audit

### May include

- governed dependency records for a declared scope;
- diagnostic responses mapped to layers and exposure types;
- evidence-led wording that records gaps as insufficient rather than weakness;
- explicit audit scope, evidence limits, and freshness requirements.

### Must not include

- certification of independence, resilience, compliance, or readiness;
- unsupported operational claims or deployment guarantees;
- a universal sovereignty score or ranking derived from audit responses;
- treatment of diagnostic hints as purchase, investment, or deployment advice.

## Premium Reference Dossier

### May include

- deep reference structure on a bounded topic within the ontology;
- sourced claims with registry IDs where appropriate;
- methodology transparency and visible corrections policy;
- durable institutional reference material with declared update boundaries.

### Must not include

- product reviews, affiliate conclusions, or sponsored rankings;
- speculative supply, capacity, or market-share narratives without evidence;
- investment advice or procurement direction;
- presentation of analysis as official government or industry endorsement.

## Ontology and Methodology Licensing

### May include

- licensed use of ontology layers, exposure types, diagnostic structure, and
  standard language under written terms;
- required attribution, evidence standards, and prohibited derivative rules;
- documented update and maintenance expectations.

### Must not include

- implied official accreditation or third-party endorsement;
- permission to strip claim classes, confidence labels, or limitations;
- resale of ungoverned score, ranking, or benchmark products;
- misrepresentation of licensed materials as verified operational findings.

## Universal Requirements

Every premium output must:

- declare scope, geographic boundaries, and time horizon;
- carry an `as_of` date for material findings;
- label confidence based on evidence quality and completeness;
- disclose limitations, conflicts, and stale-evidence risk;
- distinguish evidence from interpretation;
- preserve the prohibition on collapsing layered exposure into one headline
  number.

## Prohibited Conclusions and Advice Categories

No premium pathway may produce:

- sovereignty scores or assignments;
- public or private rankings;
- benchmarks or maturity grades;
- certifications or guarantees;
- investment recommendations;
- legal interpretations or compliance verdicts;
- procurement recommendations;
- national-security guidance;
- affiliate, advertising, or sponsored-conclusion language.

## Insufficient Evidence Rule

When evidence is incomplete, unattributable, stale, or outside scope, the correct
output is `insufficient` confidence and no operational finding. Premium work
must not compensate for missing evidence with rhetorical certainty.
