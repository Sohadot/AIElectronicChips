Governance · Evidence and claims

Methodology

Credible compute sovereignty analysis begins by separating evidence, interpretation, confidence, and unknowns.

The methodology applies the project's source and claim policies to every future reference record, diagnostic output, and commissioned brief.

Reference framingThis page defines an analytical layer. It does not make a current operational claim about any entity.
01

Source hierarchy

Primary, dated, and attributable evidence is preferred: laws and official notices, formal company disclosures, technical standards, patents, peer-reviewed research, and official institutional publications.

02

Claim classes

Material statements are classified as verified fact, attributed statement, estimate, analysis, or unknown. Analysis must remain visibly distinct from the evidence used to derive it.

03

Confidence and freshness

Findings use High, Medium, Low, or Insufficient confidence and carry an as-of date. Changing policy, capacity, ownership, and access conditions require review before reuse.

04

Corrections and conflict

Credible conflicting sources are disclosed rather than averaged away. Material errors require a visible correction describing what changed, when, and why.
