Evidence layer · Registry protocol and source boundaries

Source Registry

A governed index of official and high-quality sources that may support future reference pages, dependency records, briefs, and audits.

Registration records source identity, scope, freshness, and limitations. It does not approve every statement in a source or turn source material into a finding.

Evidence boundaryA registered source can support a scoped claim only after claim-specific review. Sources do not automatically create findings.
01

Purpose

The registry preserves the metadata needed to assess whether a source is attributable, current enough, and relevant to a specific layer and claim type. It supports governed research; it is not a conclusions database.

02

Source hierarchy

Government rules, regulations, official notices, and official datasets are preferred where applicable. Company filings and annual reports, technical standards, peer-reviewed research, and official institutional reports follow according to the claim being tested.

03

Registry status

Active means source identity and metadata have been reviewed. Pending review means useful material requires further verification before detailed claim use. Retired sources remain historical records and cannot support new findings.

04

Freshness and confidence

Every record carries an access date and temporal scope. Confidence is assigned to a supported finding after evidence review; it is not inherited from a publisher name or registry status.

05

Scope and limitations

Each source is bounded by supported claim types, geographic scope, temporal scope, ontology layers, and stated use limitations. General policy or industry material cannot establish entity-level exposure by itself.

06

Conflict and uncertainty

Credible conflicts are disclosed rather than averaged away. Stale, incomplete, or conflicting evidence can reduce confidence or require an insufficient finding.

07

Seed registry

The initial registry contains ten official or high-quality records spanning controls, semiconductor policy, company disclosures, fabrication, packaging, interconnect, power and thermal constraints, and deployment context. Two remain pending review and are not treated as active evidence.
