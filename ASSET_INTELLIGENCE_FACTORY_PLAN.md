# Asset Intelligence Factory Plan

## Mission

AIElectronicChips.com turns governed evidence into reusable intelligence about
AI compute dependency. The factory model emphasizes repeatable research
artifacts rather than content volume.

## Inputs

- governed source records;
- ontology-aligned dependency records;
- dated policy and access constraints;
- infrastructure and capacity evidence;
- clearly labeled unknowns and conflicting sources.

## Production Pipeline

1. Define the entity, decision, and time horizon.
2. Collect sources according to `SOURCE_POLICY.md`.
3. Extract claims and classify them under `CLAIM_POLICY.md`.
4. Map dependencies to the sovereignty ontology.
5. Review evidence quality, conflicts, and freshness.
6. Produce a bounded artifact with sources and confidence.
7. Schedule review where the underlying conditions can change.

## Initial Products

- **Compute Dependency Exposure Map:** a diagnostic view of dependency layers.
- **Compute Sovereignty Brief:** a concise, decision-oriented assessment.
- **Chip Dependency Audit:** a scoped review of an organization or sector.
- **Infrastructure Readiness Brief:** an assessment of deployment constraints.
- **Premium Reference Dossier:** a deep reference artifact on a specific layer.

## First Tool Boundary

The first public tool is a **Chip Dependency Map**, not a sovereignty score. It
collects a small set of structured answers and returns:

- dependency layers;
- exposure types;
- confidence level;
- a suggested brief type.

The output is preliminary and must never be presented as a definitive judgment.

## Quality Gates

No artifact is published without scope, `as_of` date, sources, confidence, and
review ownership. Automation may assist collection and formatting but may not
invent evidence or approve high-risk claims.
