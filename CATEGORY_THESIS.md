# Category Thesis

## Foundation

**AI chips are the physical substrate of compute sovereignty.**

AIElectronicChips.com defines and develops the category of **AI Chip
Sovereignty Intelligence**: the disciplined study of who can design, produce,
access, power, deploy, and sustain the physical systems required for AI
compute.

The asset is not a chip news site, product-review publication, affiliate
catalog, or general AI blog. It is a governed reference layer for understanding
compute dependency and strategic exposure.

## Core Question

Who has the practical ability to operate AI compute, under what dependencies,
and with what constraints?

Answering that question requires analysis across:

- chip design and intellectual property;
- fabrication capacity and manufacturing equipment;
- advanced packaging and high-bandwidth memory;
- interconnects, power, cooling, and deployment capacity;
- supply-chain alternatives and concentration;
- export controls, licensing, and other access constraints.

## Category Boundary

AIElectronicChips.com may explain technology, companies, markets, policy, and
infrastructure only when they clarify compute sovereignty. It must not publish:

- commodity news or lightly rewritten announcements;
- consumer buying guides or GPU reviews;
- unsupported geopolitical or security claims;
- shallow company rankings;
- volume content without sources.

## Strategic Sequence

The asset develops in this order:

`Thesis -> Ontology -> Standard -> Protocol -> Tool -> Reference Layer -> Interface -> Buyer Logic -> Monetization`

Each later layer must remain consistent with the governed foundation established
before it.
