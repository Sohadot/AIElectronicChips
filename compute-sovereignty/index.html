Compute Sovereignty

Compute sovereignty is the practical ability to operate and sustain compute under understood dependencies.

It is not a claim of total independence. It is a disciplined way to ask which capabilities an entity controls, which it accesses through others, and where substitution is constrained or unknown.

Reference framingThis page defines an analytical layer. It does not make a current operational claim about any entity.
00

Reference summary

Definition. Compute sovereignty is the practical ability to operate and sustain AI compute under understood dependencies across all eight ontology layers.

Compute sovereignty relationship. This page defines the category the entire reference system serves. It is the conceptual entry point before layer-specific analysis.

Boundary. This page does not assign sovereignty or non-sovereignty to any entity. Material claims require dated evidence through the source registry and methodology.

01

A layered condition

Compute becomes operational only when design, fabrication, packaging, memory, interconnect, power, controls, and deployment align. Control at one layer does not erase exposure at another.

02

Reference framing, not a verdict

This surface defines questions and dependency categories. It does not make current claims about a country, company, facility, or supply chain without dated and attributable evidence.

03

A decision-oriented question

The useful question is not whether an entity is simply sovereign or dependent. It is: dependent on what, through whom, under which conditions, with what alternatives, and at what confidence?
