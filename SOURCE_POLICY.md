# Source Policy

## Principle

AIElectronicChips.com prioritizes primary, dated, and attributable evidence.
Every material claim must be traceable to sources appropriate to its risk.

## Source Hierarchy

Use sources in this order where practicable:

1. laws, regulations, official notices, license rules, and government datasets;
2. securities filings, audited reports, and formal company disclosures;
3. technical standards, patents, and peer-reviewed research;
4. official institutional and intergovernmental publications;
5. reputable industry research with disclosed methodology;
6. high-quality journalism used as context or as a pointer to primary evidence.

Anonymous commentary, social posts, unsourced aggregations, and generated text
are not evidence.

## Required Source Metadata

Each source record should include:

- title, publisher, and canonical URL;
- publication or effective date;
- date accessed;
- source type and relevant ontology layer;
- the exact claim or field it supports;
- geographic and temporal scope;
- archive link where legally and technically practical.

## Freshness

Sources must carry an `as_of` date. Claims affected by policy, access,
production capacity, ownership, or company strategy require review before
reuse. Older sources may establish history but must not silently represent
current conditions.

## Conflicts and Corrections

Conflicting reliable sources must be disclosed, not averaged away. Material
errors must be corrected promptly with a visible note explaining what changed,
when, and why.
