Silicon Sovereignty Stack

The Silicon Sovereignty Stack maps the dependencies that make AI compute possible.

The stack is an analytical ontology, not a maturity ranking. It prevents a single capability from being mistaken for control of the complete compute substrate.

Reference framingThis page defines an analytical layer. It does not make a current operational claim about any entity.
Ontology view

Eight connected dependency layers

  1. 01DesignArchitecture, IP, tools, and design capability.↗
  2. 02FabricationManufacturing access, process capability, and yield.↗

  7. 07ControlsRules, licenses, scope, and effective dates.↗
  8. 08DeploymentInstallation, operation, maintenance, and resilience.↗
01

Read across layers

Each layer has its own evidence, constraints, suppliers, permissions, and substitutes. A credible assessment records these differences instead of collapsing them into one headline.

02

Read between layers

Exposure often appears at transitions: a design that needs unavailable fabrication, a package constrained by memory, or installed systems limited by power and cooling.

03

No universal score

The stack supports structured diagnosis and comparison within a declared scope. It does not produce an arbitrary universal sovereignty score.
