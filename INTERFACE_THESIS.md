# Interface Thesis

## Visual Foundation

The interface embodies the **Silicon Sovereignty Stack**. It should feel like a
precise analytical instrument whose structure explains dependencies, not a
generic technology publication or science-fiction dashboard.

## Visual Language

- deep black, graphite, and silicon gray establish the substrate;
- subtle copper traces communicate connection and dependency;
- electric blue is reserved for active evidence, selection, or disclosure;
- micro-grids, wafer cross-sections, and circuit traces provide structure;
- motion reveals transitions, bottlenecks, and exposure between layers.

Every color and interaction must have a defined analytical function.

## Interaction Model

The primary interface is layered:

1. Design
2. Fabrication
3. Packaging
4. Memory
5. Interconnect
6. Power and Thermal
7. Controls
8. Deployment

Users should move between overview, dependency, evidence, and confidence. A
visual element must never imply more certainty than the underlying sources.

## Prohibited Patterns

- decorative neon, gaming aesthetics, or fake science-fiction effects;
- animated complexity that does not convey information;
- generic stock imagery of chips, robots, or glowing brains;
- arbitrary scores without methodology and evidence;
- hidden source context or confidence labels.

## Accessibility and Performance

The interface must remain legible without motion, usable by keyboard, responsive
on small screens, and fast on constrained connections. Analytical authority
depends on clarity and restraint.
