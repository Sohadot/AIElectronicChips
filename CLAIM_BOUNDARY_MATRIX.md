# Claim Boundary Matrix

## Purpose

This matrix governs what AIElectronicChips.com may state, how it must be
supported, and what remains prohibited. It extends `CLAIM_POLICY.md` and
applies to public reference pages, dependency records, briefs, audits, and
dossiers.

The asset must not become an AI chips content site. It must remain a governed
reference layer for the physical substrate of compute sovereignty.

## Claim Categories

| Category | Rule | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Permitted without source** | General definitions, ontology framing, methodology, and explicit boundaries that do not assert current facts about an entity | "Compute sovereignty is interpreted through layered dependency exposure." |
| **Permitted only with source** | Verified facts, attributed statements, legal scope, technical specifications, and institutional summaries within source scope | "Regulation (EU) 2023/1781 entered into force on 2023-09-21." |
| **Permitted only with confidence qualifier** | Estimates, analysis, projections, institutional recommendations, and partial findings | "Medium confidence: attributed company disclosure suggests..." |
| **Prohibited currently** | Operational claims, rankings, forecasts, or entity judgments not yet supported by scoped primary evidence | "Country X leads in AI chips." |
| **Prohibited always** | Sovereignty assignment, public scores, investment or legal advice, affiliate framing, and purchasable verdicts | "This chip is the best." |

## Permitted Without Source

The following may appear as reference framing without a registered source:

- category and thesis definitions;
- ontology layer names, governing questions, and exposure types;
- methodology, claim classes, and confidence labels;
- explicit statements of what the asset does not claim;
- model-preview boundaries for non-operational tools;
- links to governed protocols and registries.

These statements must remain definitional. They must not smuggle in current
facts about a company, country, facility, or product.

## Permitted Only With Source

The following require a registered source or primary evidence within scope:

- export-control scope, effective dates, and legal text references;
- government semiconductor strategies and program announcements;
- company filings, annual reports, and official technical disclosures;
- technical-standard release scope;
- institutional report summaries and modelled projections;
- product-level technical characteristics when attributed to the disclosing party.

Registry presence does not approve every statement in a source. Claim use remains
source-specific, dated, and bounded.

## Permitted Only With Confidence Qualifier

The following require an explicit confidence label and limitations:

- analysis derived from multiple scoped facts;
- estimates and modelled projections;
- institutional recommendations;
- partial dependency exposure where evidence is incomplete;
- cross-layer synthesis within a declared scope.

`Insufficient` confidence means no operational finding.

## Prohibited Currently

The following are out of scope until supported by scoped primary evidence and
governed review:

- country or company league tables;
- statements that a company "will win" or "will dominate";
- claims that a country or entity is sovereign or non-sovereign;
- "best chip," "best HBM," or similar superiority claims;
- market-wide availability, allocation, or production-capacity conclusions;
- supplier ranking or future-supply narratives;
- geopolitical intent assignments without evidence;
- news-cycle commentary used as foundational evidence.

## Prohibited Always

The following are never permitted on the public surface or in governed outputs:

- public sovereignty scores or maturity grades;
- benchmarks presented as universal rankings;
- certifications, guarantees, or readiness verdicts;
- investment, legal, procurement, or national-security advice;
- affiliate, product-review, or sponsored-conclusion language;
- predetermined findings or purchasable verdicts;
- checkout, pricing, or self-serve commercial conversion on the reference site.

## Sensitive Claim Examples

| Claim type | Status | Requirement |
| --- | --- | --- |
| "AI chips are the physical substrate of compute sovereignty." | Permitted without source | Thesis definition |
| "HBM bandwidth is a system dependency." | Permitted without source | Layer framing |
| "The EU Chips Act establishes a legal framework." | Permitted only with source | Regulation record + text review |
| "Company X disclosed risk factor Y in its filing." | Permitted only with source + attribution | Filing record + scoped quote |
| "Facility readiness is likely in 2027." | Prohibited currently | Needs primary evidence; not a reference-layer default |
| "Country A is compute sovereign." | Prohibited always | Sovereignty assignment |
| "NVIDIA is the best AI chip vendor." | Prohibited always | Ranking / product-review framing |

## Review Requirements

Every material claim must state or imply:

- claim class;
- source reference where required;
- geographic and temporal scope;
- confidence where required;
- limitations and `as_of` dating for findings.

Conflicts must be disclosed. Missing evidence must remain `unknown` or
`insufficient`, not negative inference.
