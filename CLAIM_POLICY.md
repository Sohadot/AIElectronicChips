# Claim Policy

## Standard

Claims must be specific, sourced, bounded, and proportionate to the available
evidence. AIElectronicChips.com distinguishes facts, estimates, analysis, and
unknowns.

## Claim Classes

- **Verified fact:** directly supported by reliable primary evidence.
- **Attributed statement:** a claim made by a named source, clearly attributed.
- **Estimate:** a quantified or directional assessment with disclosed method.
- **Analysis:** an interpretation derived from cited facts and stated reasoning.
- **Unknown:** information that cannot be responsibly established.

## High-Risk Claims

Claims involving national security, geopolitical intent, export restrictions,
supply-chain disruption, company misconduct, or strategic vulnerability require
heightened review. They must:

- use precise language and avoid implying intent without evidence;
- cite primary sources wherever available;
- state jurisdiction, scope, effective date, and known exceptions;
- distinguish announced policy from implemented policy;
- disclose uncertainty and credible conflicting evidence.

## Prohibited Practices

- presenting inference as fact;
- using a single weak source for a consequential claim;
- making timeless statements about changing policy or capacity;
- assigning motives to governments or companies without evidence;
- turning missing data into a negative conclusion;
- publishing generated citations or unverifiable quotations.

## Confidence Labels

Use `High`, `Medium`, `Low`, or `Insufficient` confidence. Confidence reflects
evidence quality and completeness, not rhetorical certainty. Low-confidence
findings must be visibly labeled; insufficient evidence must result in no
finding.
