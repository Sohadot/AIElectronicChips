Reference layers · 01–03

Design, Fabrication, and Packaging

Design, fabrication, and packaging are distinct capabilities with distinct dependencies.

Treating them as one activity hides where control, access, capacity, and substitution actually reside. The sovereignty stack separates them so evidence can be attached to the correct layer.

Reference framingThis page defines an analytical layer. It does not make a current operational claim about any entity.
01

Design

Design analysis considers architecture, intellectual property, tools, talent, verification, and the rights needed to turn intent into a manufacturable design.

02

Fabrication

Fabrication analysis considers manufacturing access, process capability, equipment, materials, capacity, yield, and the conditions under which production is available.

03

Packaging

Packaging analysis considers die integration, substrates, assembly, test, thermal design, and the capacity required to turn manufactured dies into deployable systems.
